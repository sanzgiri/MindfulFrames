**Day 3-4: Gather Essentials**

**You Need (Absolute Minimum):**
1. **Camera**
   - Phone camera? Perfect.
   - Old point-and-shoot? Great.
   - DSLR/mirrorless? Fine, but not necessary.
   - **Most important:** A camera you'll actually carry.

2. **Journal**
   - Notebook from dollar store works
   - Digital notes on phone works
   - Fancy journal nice but not needed
   - **Most important:** Something you'll actually use.

3. **Rain Protection (You're in Portland!)**
   - Plastic ziplock bag for camera
   - Rain jacket for you
   - Umbrella
   - Waterproof shoes
   - **Most important:** Don't let rain stop you.

**Nice to Have (But Not Required):**
- Tripod (for self-portraits)
- Extra storage (memory card or phone storage)
- Lens cloth (for rain spots)
- Comfortable camera bag
- Headphones for meditation/music

**Don't Buy Anything Expensive Yet!**
Wait until week 4 to see what you actually need.

---

**Day 5-6: Test Your Setup**

**Camera Basics (If You Don't Know):**

**Phone Camera:**
- Open camera app
- Tap screen to focus
- Use volume button to shoot (less shake)
- Turn on grid lines (Settings > Camera > Grid)
- Learn where photos save
- That's enough to start!

**Any Camera:**
- Turn it on (charge it first!)
- Figure out how to take a photo
- Learn how to review photos
- Learn how to transfer photos to computer/phone
- Done! Everything else you'll learn by doing.

**Take 10 Test Photos:**
- 5 inside your home
- 5 outside
- Just practice pressing button
- Don't judge quality
- Get comfortable with camera

## WEEK 1: YOUR FIRST WEEK (Oct 25-31)

### Saturday, Oct 25: COURSE STARTS!

**Morning (9am-11am): Your First Excursion**

**Go to Progress Ridge TownSquare:**
1. Drive to Progress Ridge (5 min)
2. Get coffee at Ava Roasteria
3. Find a bench overlooking Progress Lake
4. Sit for 10 minutes just looking (don't photograph yet!)
5. Notice: What do you see? What draws your eye?

**Then:**
6. Walk slowly around the lake (20 minutes)
7. Stop when something catches your attention
8. Before photographing, look at it for 30 seconds
9. Ask: "What am I really seeing here?"
10. Take ONE photograph
11. Repeat this 10 times around the lake
12. Goal: 10 photographs in 1 hour

**What You're Practicing:**
- Slowing down
- Noticing what attracts you
- Pausing before shooting
- Beginning beginner's mind

**After:**
- Get a pastry at La Provence if you want
- Review your 10 images
- Choose your favorite
- Journal: What surprised me?

---

**Sunday, Oct 26: Murrayhill Park Practice**

**Morning (8am-9am):**
1. Walk to Murrayhill Park (10 min)
2. Find a spot on the wooded trail
3. Stand still for 5 minutes
4. Notice: Birds, wind, light through trees, sounds
5. This is meditation - you're present

**Then:**
6. Walk the trail slowly
7. Photograph anything that catches your eye
8. No rules, no judgment
9. 10-15 images
10. Goal: Practice seeing with fresh eyes

**Journal Prompt:**
- One thing I never noticed before
- How did it feel to slow down?
- What's difficult about this practice?

---

**Monday-Friday, Oct 27-31: Daily Practice Begins**

**Each Morning (Before Work/Life - 15 minutes):**

**7:00am: Window Meditation**
1. Sit by window with coffee/tea
2. Look outside for 5 minutes
3. Don't name what you see ("tree, car, sky")
4. Just see colors, shapes, light
5. Notice when you start naming - return to just seeing

**7:05am: Choose One Object**
Today you'll photograph ONE object in your home ten different ways.

Monday: Coffee mug
Tuesday: Plant
Wednesday: Your hands
Thursday: Light through window
Friday: Any object that calls to you

**7:10am: 10 Photographs**
- Get close, step back
- Try different angles (high, low, side)
- Different light (morning, evening, lamp)
- Different framing (tight, loose, cropped)
- Goal: Find 10 ways to see one thing

**7:15am: Review & Journal**
- Look at your 10 images
- Choose favorite
- Write one sentence: "This image works because..."

**Each Evening (After Work - 15 minutes):**

**Walk to Murrayhill Park** (or neighborhood loop if time short)
1. Walk slowly (half your normal speed)
2. Notice 3 things you never noticed before
3. Photograph them
4. Return home

**Before Bed:**
- Review day's images (3 from evening walk + 10 from morning)
- Choose best image of the day
- Write: "Today I learned..."

---

**Friday Evening, Oct 31: First Week Complete!**

**Week 1 Review:**
1. Look at ALL your images from the week
2. How many did you take? (Around 100?)
3. Choose your 10 favorites
4. Create folder "Week 1 - Best"
5. Save those 10 there

**Reflection Questions:**
- What surprised me this week?
- What was harder than expected?
- What was easier than expected?
- Did I stick to daily practice? (If not, why not?)
- What will I adjust for next week?
- Am I starting to see differently? How?

**Celebrate:**
- You completed Week 1!
- You took ~100 photographs mindfully
- You established a daily practice
- You're learning to see
- Pat yourself on the back!

## COMMON FIRST WEEK CHALLENGES

### "I Don't Have Time!"

**Solutions:**
- Wake up 20 minutes earlier (yes, you can)
- Replace morning social media scroll with practice
- Weekend excursion is non-negotiable - protect it
- Remember: You're learning to be present in life you already have
- This practice SAVES time by making life more meaningful

**Minimum Viable Practice:** 10 minutes daily
- 5 min window meditation
- 5 min three photographs
- Even this is enough if consistent

---

### "My Photos Are Terrible!"

**Reality Check:**
- This isn't about good photos
- It's about practicing seeing
- "Terrible" photos teach as much as "good" ones
- You're a beginner - beginner work looks like beginner work
- That's normal and okay
- Keep going

**Reframe:**
- Instead of "good photo," ask "did I pay attention?"
- Instead of "Instagram-worthy," ask "did I notice something?"
- Instead of comparing to others, ask "am I growing?"

---

### "I Feel Silly/Self-Conscious"

**Normal!**
- Everyone feels this
- Photographing ordinary things feels weird at first
- Standing still in park feels awkward
- It passes with practice
- Your discomfort is ego resisting change
- Keep going anyway

**Helps:**
- Go early morning (fewer people)
- Remember: No one is watching
- Phone cameras are subtle
- You're allowed to photograph anything
- Your practice matters more than others' opinions

---

### "I Don't Feel Anything/See Anything Special"

**Also Normal!**
- Mindfulness is subtle
- Changes accumulate slowly
- You might not notice shifts until Week 4-6
- Trust the process
- Keep practicing even when it feels pointless
- The seeing develops quietly underneath

**Check:**
- Are you rushing? Slow down more.
- Are you judging? Let go of judgment.
- Are you comparing? Stop comparing.
- Are you present? Return to presence.

---

### "I Missed a Day (or Three)"

**It's Okay!**
- Don't quit because you missed days
- Just start again
- No guilt, no shame
- Consistency matters more than perfection
- Every day is new beginning

**Restart Protocol:**
1. Acknowledge gap without drama
2. Identify what blocked you (too ambitious? wrong time? external crisis?)
3. Adjust plan slightly
4. Begin again tomorrow
5. No starting over - pick up where you are

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Do I need to start on Oct 25?**
A: No, but starting with the group provides seasonal context and potential community. You can start any time and adapt seasonal references.

**Q: What if I can't do Portland locations?**
A: Adapt to your location. The principles work anywhere. Just find: daily nearby spot (your "Murrayhill Park"), weekly destination (your "Progress Ridge"), and occasional weekend locations.

**Q: Can I use filters/editing?**
A: Yes, but try shooting some images without editing. The practice is about seeing, not perfecting. Light editing okay, heavy manipulation moves away from mindfulness.

**Q: How do I know if I'm "doing it right"?**
A: Are you practicing regularly? Are you paying attention? Are you learning about yourself and your seeing? Then you're doing it right. There's no wrong way to practice presence.

**Q: Should I share my images publicly?**
A: Only if you want to. This practice is for you. Sharing is optional. Many students keep their work private. Others find sharing motivating. Your choice.

**Q: What if I can't afford the recommended books?**
A: Library! Portland Public Library has photography books. Also, the course materials you have are complete without buying anything else.

**Q: Can I do this with my phone camera?**
A: Yes! Phone cameras are excellent. Most professional photographers carry phones as backup. Camera matters far less than seeing.

**Q: Is this religious/spiritual?**
A: No religious affiliation required. Mindfulness has Buddhist roots but is practiced secularly. You can approach this as spiritual practice or simply as attention training. Your choice.
